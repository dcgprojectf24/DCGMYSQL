About the Hawaiian & Pacific Collections

Description

The Hawaiian and Pacific Collections are leading research collections about Hawai’i and the broader Pacific Islands region. These collections support the teaching and research mission of the University, as well as the research needs of local, national, and international scholars.

The Hawaiian Collection is a comprehensive collection of retrospective and current materials pertaining to Hawai’i, spanning from the 18th century to the present. The Pacific Collection focuses on the island regions of Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia, with strengths in government documents, ethnographic studies, and voyaging accounts.

The collections are housed in closed stacks on the 5th floor of Hamilton Library. Materials can be requested online at any time through OneSearch and are generally available 15 minutes after the request.
