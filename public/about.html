About Our Project

Our Mission

The Database Consulting Group (DCG) is dedicated to transforming Hamilton Library's Hawaiian and Pacific Collections into a centralized, efficient, and user-friendly database. By modernizing the current Excel-based system, we aim to enhance data accessibility, reduce inefficiencies, and ensure the long-term preservation of invaluable archival materials.

What We Offer

Our project provides a web-integrated database system tailored to the needs of library staff and researchers. The system includes advanced search capabilities, robust reporting tools, and secure user authentication to streamline cataloging, retrieval, and analysis of the library's extensive collection.

Data and Content We Manage

Our database is designed to handle a wide range of specialized content, including:

  • Books: Detailed metadata such as titles, authors, publication dates, and descriptions.
  • Maps: Geographic data showing colonial versus indigenous languages and location-specific details.
  • Audiovisual Materials: Formats, descriptions, and associated creators or titles.
  • Archival Records: Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands (TTPI) indexes, including land records and historical documents.
  • Serials and Articles: Titles, authors, and sponsoring bodies for single-issue serial collections.
